DIY: Designer saree with fabric painting and lace work

by Priyanka

2018-08-20

Humans have been painting on fabric for thousands of years. Fabric painting is one of the ways to add colors and life to your boring, dull and plain clothes. There are number of techniques to paint on fabric.

I always had a fascination about fabric painting. When I was in 7th standard, I first tried fabric painting on my Kurti and then I went on trying different techniques of fabric painting. One of my favorite techniques is free hand fabric painting. For this technique, you don't need to trace any design on fabric. You just need to fill brush with good amount of color and paint away. But before starting free hand painting on fabric, you must have design in your mind. You can use single color for this or can use 2-3 colors to have shading effect. Some basic tips for fabric painting:

  1. Pre-washing the fabric is not always required but it is recommended to remove any starch or sizing added during manufacturing.
  2. Iron the fabric after washing to paint smoothly on it.
  3. Always use cardboard or notebook to place underneath the fabric to prevent it from bleeding through the fabric.
  4. If you are not confident about directly painting on fabric, you can always trace a design on fabric by fabric chalk or using carbon paper.
  5. If you made any mistake while painting, don't worry, try lifting it out immediately by dry brush and then clean it with wet wipes or wet cloth.
  6. Even if color gets dried out before cleaning it, you can use Nail paint remover to remove dried paint. Just dip cotton ear bud in nail paint remover and rub it on cloth until the color gets removed from the cloth.
  7. Wait for 24 hours after painting. And then you can iron it from back side of painting.

This is my free hand painting on saree. I got this saree as a gift from my sister-in-law on last Diwali. And from then I was thinking whether to make it heavy designer saree by attaching Jardosi border or make it simple just by painting on it. And I decided to paint on it as it would give me chance to explore my creativity.

Flowers are such a divine creations. They instantly gives you colorful life and positive energy. So I thought of painting flowers on saree. This is light lemon yellow colored soft chiffon saree. I have used Acrylic Deep Bril Purple-64 ,Camel Fabrica Acrylic ultra White color and Faber Castle 6 no brush for flowers and Acrylic Sap Green-21 color and Faber Castle 0 no brush for leaves. I repeated the design randomly all over the saree.

But after I completed my painting work, I realised that this saree is very light in weight. So it will be difficult to drape it properly as it will not get a nice fall. So I attached a simple pearl border to add little weight to saree. And here is my saree ready with fabric painting and lace work at home.

I wore this saree for one small family function with hot pink bot neck blouse and long kundan earrings,some bangles and then completed my look with off white colored heels.

You can always change something simple, plain into an attractive piece with little creativity and efforts. Let me know what you all think about this. Have a happy week ahead. XOXO!
